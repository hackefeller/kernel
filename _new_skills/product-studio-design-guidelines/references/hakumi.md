# Hakumi Reference

## Identity

Hakumi is a digital zen garden for productivity. It weaves together notes, calendars, AI conversations, and goals so everything flows as one system.

Name meaning:
- `haku` / 帛: silk, smoothness, premium refinement
- `kumi` / 組: to weave, assemble, structure
- Preferred script: 帛組
- Narrative: the pure weave

Use kanji over katakana when representing the Japanese name. The `haku` character refers to silk and avoids unwanted color associations.

## Visual Identity

Core palette:
- Absolute black
- Pure white
- Tonal grays

Functional accents:
- Red: urgent alerts and overdue tasks
- Green: completion and goal milestones
- Yellow: suggestions and active AI processing

Negative space is part of the product. Hakumi interfaces should feel airy even when they contain a lot of information.

## Product Model

Hakumi integrates:
- Notes
- Calendars
- AI conversations
- Goals

The core product question is how these surfaces weave together without making the user manage four separate systems.

When designing Hakumi flows, prioritize:
- Capture without friction
- Retrieval without search anxiety
- Calendar awareness without noise
- Goal progress that feels calm rather than managerial
- AI that acts where useful and waits where user agency matters

## Voice

North star: calm, minimal, efficient.

Voice guidelines:
- Prefer short, direct sentences.
- Use plain verbs.
- Avoid hype, cheerleading, and theatrical reassurance.
- Frame suggestions as options, not orders.
- Make errors clear and actionable.
- Keep AI responses compact unless the user asks for expansion.

Good patterns:
- "This looks overdue."
- "I found three related notes."
- "Want to move this to tomorrow?"
- "I could summarize the thread or extract tasks."

Avoid:
- "Great job crushing your goals!"
- "Let me revolutionize your workflow."
- "You absolutely need to..."
- Long explanations before the action.

## Interface Feel

Hakumi should feel like a quiet workspace, not a dashboard trying to impress. Use hierarchy, spacing, and semantic state rather than decoration.

Useful product metaphors:
- Weaving
- Threading
- Flow
- Silk
- Garden
- Quiet structure

Do not overuse metaphors in visible copy. They should inform design decisions more than appear as repeated language.

## Open Source Gaps

The source app spec and tone docs are currently stubs. Treat the above as first-principles guidance from the existing identity and roadmap, not as a complete product specification.
