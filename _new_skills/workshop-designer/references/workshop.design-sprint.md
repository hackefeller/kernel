# Design Sprint

**Duration:** 5 days (Monday-Friday)\
**Best for:** Testing big ideas without big commitments\
**Investment:** $25,000 - $40,000

---

## Overview

Based on the Google Ventures design sprint methodology, adapted for real-world constraints.

Got a big idea but worried about investing months only to learn it doesn't work? The design sprint compresses months of work into one week. By Friday, you'll have a realistic prototype and real user feedback.

This is the fastest way to answer critical business questions through design, prototyping, and testing with users.

---

## The Week at a Glance

**Monday:** Map the problem and choose a focus area\
**Tuesday:** Sketch competing solutions individually\
**Wednesday:** Decide on the best solution and storyboard\
**Thursday:** Build a realistic prototype\
**Friday:** Test with 5+ real users and learn

---

## Day-by-Day Breakdown

### Monday: Map

_Understand the problem space and choose what to focus on_

**Morning:**

- Set long-term goal
- Map the challenge
- Ask the experts (interview stakeholders)

**Afternoon:**

- HMW (How Might We) notes
- Organize HMWs
- Choose target customer and event
- Define sprint questions

**Output:** A map of the customer journey with a clear target focus area

---

### Tuesday: Sketch

_Generate competing solution concepts_

**Morning:**

- Lightning demos (review existing solutions)
- Divide or swarm (decide team structure)

**Afternoon:**

- Four-step sketch process:
  - Notes (20 minutes)
  - Ideas (20 minutes)
  - Crazy 8s (8 minutes)
  - Solution sketch (30+ minutes)

**Output:** Multiple competing solution concepts to evaluate

---

### Wednesday: Decide

_Choose the best solution and create a storyboard_

**Morning:**

- Art museum (review all solutions)
- Heat map voting
- Speed critique
- Straw poll

**Afternoon:**

- Supervote (decision-maker chooses)
- Storyboard the winning solution
- Plan the prototype

**Output:** A detailed storyboard ready to prototype

---

### Thursday: Prototype

_Build something realistic enough to test_

**All Day:**

- Assign roles (maker, stitcher, writer, asset collector)
- Build realistic prototype in Figma/similar
- Review and refine
- Prepare for Friday's test

**Output:** A clickable prototype that feels real

---

### Friday: Test

_Get feedback from real users_

**Morning & Afternoon:**

- Interview 5+ users (one-on-one, 60 mins each)
- Team observes and takes notes
- Look for patterns

**End of Day:**

- Debrief as a team
- Identify patterns
- Decide next steps

**Output:** Validated or invalidated concept with clear next steps

## What You'll Learn

**Does this solution work?**\
Do users understand it? Can they use it? Does it solve their problem?

**What should we build?**\
Which features matter most? What can we cut?

**Where do users struggle?**\
What's confusing? Where do they get stuck?

**Is this worth pursuing?**\
Should we invest in building this for real?

**What are the biggest risks?**\
What assumptions were wrong? What needs more research?
