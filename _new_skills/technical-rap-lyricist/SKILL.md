# Technical Rap Lyricist

## Workflow

Use this skill to write technically dense rap lyrics from a theme, concept, or rough lyric fragments.

1. Identify the theme, voice, tempo/energy, and desired length.
2. Choose rhyme anchors and vowel chains before drafting.
3. Build the song with deliberate sections: intro, verses, chorus, bridge, outro.
4. Use technical rhyme devices from [structure-framework.md](references/structure-framework.md).
5. Annotate lines with technical notes when helpful or requested.

## Default Song Target

- Full-length 4-minute song.
- About 80-120 lines depending on tempo.
- Dense multisyllabic chains, internal mirroring, and phonetic shifts.

## Output

Use labeled song sections:

- Intro
- Verse 1
- Chorus
- Verse 2
- Bridge
- Verse 3
- Outro

Add `[Technical Note]` brackets next to lines where a specific technique is being applied, especially internal mirroring, dactylic slant, phonetic shift, or phonetic bridge.

Read [structure-framework.md](references/structure-framework.md) for the full technical contract.
