# Pre-mortem

## 1. Missing critical user needs

- Conduct regular user research and feedback sessions
- Implement a robust feature request and prioritization system

## 2. Unreliable tech and offering

- Invest in thorough quality assurance and testing processes
- Establish a dedicated team for continuous improvement and bug fixing

## 3. Lacking courage in certain areas

- Foster a culture of calculated risk-taking and innovation
- Implement a decision-making framework that encourages bold moves

## 4. Insufficient awareness of competitors' actions and strategies

- Assign team members to monitor and report on competitor activities regularly
- Subscribe to industry newsletters and attend relevant conferences

## 5. Not moving fast enough while maintaining KPI focus

- Implement agile methodologies to increase speed and flexibility
- Set clear, measurable goals aligned with KPIs for each sprint or work cycle

## 6. Insufficient focus on traction efforts

- Develop a comprehensive marketing and growth strategy
- Allocate dedicated resources and budget for traction-building activities

## 7. Scaling too early

- Establish clear metrics and milestones for when to scale different aspects of the business
- Regularly reassess market conditions and internal readiness before scaling decisions

## 8. Spending too much time on non-impactful activities

- Implement a prioritization system (e.g., ICE scoring) for all tasks and projects
- Conduct regular reviews to eliminate or optimize low-impact activities

## 9. Not evaluating progress or taking time to reflect

- Schedule regular retrospectives and progress review meetings
- Implement a system for tracking and analyzing key performance indicators

## 10. Too much feature creep and risk of being replaced by simpler alternatives

- Establish a clear product roadmap with prioritized features based on user needs
- Regularly reassess and simplify existing features to maintain product focus

## 11. Insufficient resources and time to execute on ambitions

- Develop a realistic resource allocation plan aligned with strategic priorities
- Implement a system for tracking and optimizing resource utilization

## 12. Poor cash flow management or insufficient funding

- Implement robust financial forecasting and budgeting processes
- Explore diverse funding options and maintain relationships with potential investors

## 13. Lack of product-market fit or failure to solve a real customer problem

- Conduct thorough market research and validate product ideas before full development
- Implement a continuous feedback loop with early adopters and beta users

## 14. Ineffective leadership or team dynamics issues

- Invest in leadership development and team-building activities
- Establish clear communication channels and conflict resolution processes

## 15. Regulatory or legal challenges in your industry

- Engage legal counsel to stay informed about relevant regulations and compliance requirements
- Develop a proactive compliance strategy and regularly audit internal processes

## 16. Inability to adapt to changing market conditions or customer needs

- Implement a system for monitoring market trends and customer behavior
- Foster a culture of adaptability and continuous learning within the organization
