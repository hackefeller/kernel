# Document Contract

Use this contract to convert raw transcripts into structured research documents.

## Input

The transcript may include:

- multiple speakers
- tangents
- repetition
- incomplete thoughts
- informal language
- mixed-quality ideas

## Objectives

1. Extract all meaningful ideas, arguments, and insights.
2. Remove redundancy, filler, and conversational noise.
3. Resolve ambiguity and clarify intent where possible.
4. Organize ideas into a logical, hierarchical structure.
5. Elevate the writing to a professional, research-grade tone.
6. Preserve original meaning and nuance.

## Required Output Format

### 1. Title

Concise, descriptive, and specific.

### 2. Abstract

3-6 sentences summarizing key ideas and conclusions.

### 3. Key Concepts

Bullet list of core ideas, definitions, or principles.

### 4. Main Body

Organized into clear sections and subsections:

- use descriptive headers
- group related ideas together
- expand fragmented thoughts into complete explanations
- convert conversational fragments into formal reasoning

### 5. Arguments & Insights

Clearly articulate major arguments. Separate strong insights from weak or speculative ones.

### 6. Contradictions & Tensions

Identify disagreements, inconsistencies, or unresolved ideas.

### 7. Implicit Assumptions

Surface hidden assumptions that were not explicit.

### 8. Open Questions

List unanswered questions or areas for further exploration.

### 9. Practical Implications

Translate ideas into real-world applications where possible.

### 10. Conclusion

Synthesize final takeaways. Do not repeat the abstract.

## Transformation Rules

Do not:

- include speaker labels or dialogue format
- quote unless necessary
- preserve conversational tone
- omit important nuance for brevity

Do:

- merge duplicate ideas
- strengthen weak phrasing
- fill in missing logical steps when clearly implied
- normalize terminology across the document

## Quality Bar

The final output should:

- read like a well-written research paper or whitepaper
- be internally consistent and logically structured
- feel authored by a single, clear thinker
- be useful as a standalone document

## Optional Enhancements

Use when applicable:

- frameworks or models
- step-by-step processes
- taxonomies or classifications
- diagrams described in text
